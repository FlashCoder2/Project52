Origami is a design made out of a single peice of paper. The paper is folded many times to make a certain creation. It could be an animal, food, or an fictional character. For example, the Red Sea Urchin made by Hans Birkeland was made out of 913 folds that require over 2,700 creases to be manipulated.

  • Origami is a very big part in a Japenese wedding rituals
  • The first origami book was written in 1797
  • Origami was called Orikata befores which literally translates to "Folded Paper"
